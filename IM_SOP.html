Information Management
Document Suite

A standard operating procedure and reference handbook for managing information, BIM, and documentation throughout the entire project lifecycle — from assessment and tender through to asset handover. Structured around the ISO 19650 information management cycle to support consistent, auditable, and efficient delivery across all project stages and disciplines.

ISO 19650-2:2018 BEP · MIDP · MPDT CDE Governance Multi-Discipline Live Reference · 2026
§1 — Purpose of This Document
This document serves as a standard operating procedure (SOP) and reference handbook for managing information, BIM, and documentation throughout the project lifecycle. It is aligned with ISO 19650 principles and supports consistent, auditable, and efficient project delivery. It shall be maintained as a live reference and updated in line with project stage changes and contract requirements.
2
Section 2 — Framework
ISO 19650 Framework Alignment
The document set is structured around the ISO 19650 information management cycle across 7 defined stages — from assessment through to asset information delivery.
ISO 19650 Stage Stage Name Key Activities Supporting Documents
Stage 1 Assessment & Need Define information requirements, identify risks, establish project scope BEP, Project Management Plan, Risk Register
Stage 2 Invitation to Tender Define ownership and review responsibilities, issue pre-appointment BEP OCRA Matrix, Responsibility Matrix
Stage 3 Mobilisation Set up CDE, assign TIDP owners, confirm team structure and resources MIDP, TIDP Owners, Resource Planning, Organisation Chart
Stage 4 Collaborative Production Author, share, and coordinate information across disciplines in the CDE Coordination Matrix, Clash Dashboard, Issue Tracker
Stage 5 Information Delivery Deliver information containers against MIDP milestones, track compliance MPDT, Information Exchange Dashboard
Stage 6 Review & Acceptance Audit models, resolve comments, verify compliance before acceptance Model Audit Checklist, Model Quality Checklist, Comment Resolution Sheet
Stage 7 Asset Information Transfer information to asset register, archive project data, close CDE Document Management Plan, Naming Convention, Project Directory

Section 3 — Access & Accountability
Governance, Roles & Access Control
Establishes who has access to what information, who is responsible for creating and approving it, and how the team is structured to deliver it — the foundation of all ISO 19650-compliant information management.
5 Documents
3.1
ACC / CDE Access Request Form
A formal governance document used to request, approve, modify, or revoke access to the Common Data Environment. Ensures that CDE access is controlled, auditable, and aligned with project roles — preventing unauthorised access to sensitive or in-progress information containers.

3.2
OCRA Matrix
Defines Ownership, Creation, Review, and Approval responsibilities for each information container on the project. The OCRA Matrix ensures that every document and model file has a named owner, a clear creation party, defined reviewers, and a formal approver — eliminating ambiguity in information responsibility.
Coming Soon
3.3
Detailed Responsibility Matrix
Provides a discipline-wise role allocation framework covering models, documents, and coordination tasks across all project stages. Goes beyond the RACI to map specific deliverables to specific teams and individuals — serving as a practical day-to-day reference for accountability.
Coming Soon
3.4
RACI Matrix
Clarifies accountability across all BIM and project management activities using the Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed framework. Applied to all key workflows — model authoring, clash resolution, comment response, CDE management — to ensure no task falls without an owner.
Coming Soon
3.5
Organisation Chart
Defines the project team structure including reporting lines, escalation paths, and governance hierarchy. Covers all Appointed Parties, the Lead Appointed Party, and the Appointing Party — establishing who reports to whom for BIM, coordination, and information management decisions.

Section 4 — Delivery Planning
Planning & Information Delivery Management
The core planning documents that define how information will be produced, by whom, to what standard, and by when — forming the contractual and operational backbone of BIM delivery from mobilisation to construction.
6 Documents
4.1
BIM Execution Plan (BEP)
The primary governance document for the project. Defines BIM objectives, applicable standards, exchange formats, Level of Information Need, software platforms, CDE structure, and delivery workflows. Issued pre-appointment and updated post-appointment — serves as the binding agreement between all Appointed Parties on how BIM will be delivered.
Coming Soon
4.2
Master Information Delivery Plan (MIDP)
The consolidated master schedule listing all information deliverables across every discipline and stage — with submission dates, suitability codes, formats, and approval milestones. The MIDP is the single source of truth for what is due and when, drawing from all TIDPs submitted by Appointed Parties.
Coming Soon
4.3
Task Information Delivery Plans (TIDP)
Discipline-specific delivery plans owned and maintained by each Appointed Party's lead. The TIDP breaks down the MIDP requirements into task-level schedules — specifying which team member produces which deliverable, at what LOD, and by which date. Feeds directly into the MIDP and is updated at each stage gate.
Coming Soon
4.4
Model Production & Delivery Table (MPDT)
A structured schedule that lists every model file to be produced on the project — defining the responsible Appointed Party, authoring software, Level of Development per stage, and delivery milestone. The MPDT is the single source of truth for model delivery accountability, housed within the BEP.

4.5
Project Management Plan (PMP)
Defines the overarching project governance framework — covering scope, programme, change control procedures, risk management, communication protocols, and progress reporting. The PMP aligns the BIM delivery strategy with broader project management objectives and client reporting requirements.
Coming Soon
4.6
Resource Planning Register
Forecasts manpower demand and team allocation across all project stages and disciplines. Used to identify resourcing gaps early, plan recruitment or subcontracting, and ensure that delivery commitments in the MIDP and MPDT are backed by sufficient team capacity at each milestone.
Coming Soon

Section 5 — Compliance & Assurance
Quality, Audit & Compliance
The quality assurance layer that governs how models and documents are checked, audited, and verified before they are shared or accepted — ensuring every information exchange meets the standards committed to in the BEP.
3 Documents
5.1
Model Quality Checklist
A structured checklist applied by model authors before any information exchange to verify compliance with BEP requirements — covering modelling standards, element naming, parameter population, geometry accuracy, file structure, and CDE metadata. Prevents non-compliant models from reaching reviewers and consuming unnecessary review cycles.
Coming Soon
5.2
Model Audit Checklist
Used during formal model reviews conducted by the BIM Lead or Information Manager prior to each stage gate information exchange. The audit assesses BEP compliance, LOD achievement, coordination status, issue resolution progress, and data completeness — forming the basis for acceptance or rejection of the submission.
Coming Soon
5.3
OCRA Review Dashboard
A visual compliance dashboard that tracks the status of OCRA responsibilities across all information containers — displaying compliance levels, overdue review actions, and ownership gaps in real time. Enables the Information Manager to identify and escalate accountability failures before they impact delivery milestones.
Coming Soon

Section 6 — Issue Resolution
Coordination, Review & Issue Resolution
The operational layer of BIM delivery — defining how disciplines coordinate, how clashes are detected and resolved, and how design comments are tracked and closed with full auditability.
4 Documents
6.1
Coordination Matrix
Defines which disciplines coordinate with one another, at what frequency, through which platform or meeting format, and with what deliverable outcome. Eliminates coordination gaps between teams — particularly critical at MEP-structure, MEP-architecture, and civil-structure interfaces — by making coordination obligations explicit and enforceable.
Coming Soon
6.2
Clash Detection Report Dashboard
A live tracking dashboard that monitors all clash tests run against the federated model — categorised by status (open, in progress, resolved, closed), priority (critical, high, medium, low), discipline pair, and resolution owner. Enables the BIM Lead and project team to track coordination progress and identify resolution bottlenecks before they affect the programme.
↗ Read Article
6.3
Issues Tracker Dashboard
The centralised log for all design, coordination, and construction-related issues raised on the project — capturing issue type, location, priority, responsible party, target resolution date, and current status. Integrates with the CDE and clash management tools to provide a single, auditable record of every issue from identification to closure.
Coming Soon
6.4
Comment Resolution Sheet
Records all review comments received against submitted information containers — including the original comment, the author's response, the closure evidence provided, and the approver's final acceptance. Provides a complete, traceable audit trail for every design review cycle, supporting compliance with ISO 19650 information acceptance procedures.
Coming Soon

Section 7 — Reporting & Exchange
Information Exchange & Reporting
The reporting and monitoring layer that tracks the flow of information through the CDE — from initial submission to final acceptance — and maintains a live project directory as the single index of all project data.
3 Documents
7.1
Information Exchange Dashboard
A real-time monitoring dashboard that tracks the status of all information exchanges through the CDE — displaying submission counts, approval rates, rejection reasons, resubmission cycles, and overdue deliverables against MIDP milestones. Provides the Information Manager and client with a live view of delivery health across all disciplines and stages.
Coming Soon
7.2
Project Directory
The single source index of all project folders, model files, documents, registers, and dashboards — providing every team member with a clear map of where information lives in the CDE. Updated throughout the project lifecycle to reflect new deliverables, revised folder structures, and stage-gate reorganisations.
Coming Soon
7.3
Standard Excel / CDE Templates
A standardised set of Excel-based and CDE-native templates used for data exchange, progress reporting, dashboard population, and structured submissions. Ensures consistency across disciplines and reduces the risk of formatting errors or incompatible data structures when information is shared between teams or submitted to the client.
Coming Soon

Section 8 — Data Governance
Document & Data Management
The data governance layer that governs how all project documents and files are named, managed, retained, and archived — ensuring that every information container follows a consistent, ISO 19650-compliant lifecycle from creation to handover.
3 Documents
8.1
Document Management Plan (DMP)
Defines the complete document lifecycle for the project — covering status codes (S0 through S4 and A through D), approval workflows, revision control procedures, retention periods, and archiving protocols. The DMP ensures that every information container follows a consistent, controlled path from draft creation to final acceptance and long-term storage.
Coming Soon
8.2
File & Document Naming Convention
Applies the ISO 19650 container naming structure across all project data — models, drawings, documents, and reports. Defines the required fields (project, originator, volume, level, type, role, classification, number, revision), their permitted values, and the delimiter conventions required for CDE upload and automated metadata extraction.
Coming Soon
8.3
Risk Register
The live register for identifying, evaluating, mitigating, and tracking all project risks and opportunities related to BIM and information management — including CDE access risks, data security threats, coordination failures, software incompatibilities, and resource constraints. Updated throughout the project lifecycle and reviewed at each stage gate.
Coming Soon
