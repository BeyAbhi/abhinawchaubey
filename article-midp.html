Master Information
Delivery Plan

A focused guide to understanding, building, and governing the MIDP — the consolidated master schedule that defines every information deliverable on a project, when it is due, who is responsible, and to what status code it must be issued.

ISO 19650-2 BEP Component 2026 Edition
Definition
The MIDP is the consolidated master schedule of all information deliverables on a project — listing every document, model, drawing, and report that must be produced, with the responsible party, format and suitability code, and submission milestone for each. It is compiled from all Task Information Delivery Plans (TIDPs) submitted by Appointed Parties.
What?
All Deliverables
Every information container — models, drawings, documents, reports — listed with a unique reference, type, and format. Nothing is assumed to exist unless it appears in the MIDP.
Who?
Responsible Party
Each deliverable is assigned to a named Appointed Party and an individual author — making accountability for every submission explicit and traceable.
When?
Milestone Date
Submission dates are tied directly to the project programme — creating a binding delivery calendar, not a loose target list. Dates flow from the EIR and BEP commitments.
How?
Format & Status
Each deliverable specifies its file format (IFC, PDF, RVT, DWG, XLSX) and the suitability code it must achieve — from S0 work-in-progress to S4 approved for construction.

Suitability Codes
Information Status — S0 to S4
S0
Work in Progress
Being authored — not ready for sharing or review. CDE work-in-progress zone only.
S1
Suitable for Coordination
Ready for clash detection and inter-discipline coordination. Not for client review.
S2
Suitable for Information
Shared for information and reference. Not yet approved — may still change.
S3
Suitable for Review
Issued formally for client or authority review and comment. Awaiting acceptance.
S4
Approved for Construction
Fully accepted and authorised. The only status at which information may be built from.

Common Errors
5 MIDP Mistakes to Avoid
1
TIDP not collected from all parties
A MIDP compiled without complete TIDPs has gaps. Every Appointed Party must submit their TIDP before the MIDP can be finalised — no exceptions.
2
Milestones not tied to the programme
Submission dates that float independently of the project programme create false confidence. Every MIDP date must map directly to a programme milestone or stage gate.
3
Suitability codes left blank or incorrect
Missing or wrong suitability codes cause CDE submission failures. S0 to S4 must be explicitly assigned per deliverable per stage — not assumed or left open.
4
MIDP treated as a static document
The MIDP must be updated at every stage gate. Scope changes, team changes, and programme revisions all trigger a MIDP revision — it is a live schedule, not a snapshot.
5
No escalation process for overdue items
A MIDP without a defined escalation path for overdue deliverables is decoration. Every overdue item must trigger a named escalation route with a resolution deadline.
