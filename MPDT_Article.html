Model Production
& Delivery Table

A focused guide to understanding, structuring, and governing the MPDT — the primary schedule that defines what BIM models are produced, by whom, to what level, and when.

ISO 19650-2 BEP Component 2026 Edition
Definition
The MPDT is a structured schedule within the BEP that lists every model file to be produced — specifying the responsible party, authoring software, Level of Development per stage, and delivery milestone. It is the single source of truth for model delivery accountability.
What?
Models to Produce
Every model file listed with a unique ID, discipline, and scope — no ambiguity on what is in or out.
Who?
Responsible Party
Each model file assigned to a named Appointed Party — clear authorship and accountability from day one.
How?
LOD & LOI per Stage
Level of Detail (geometry) and Level of Information (data) defined per stage — not just a final target.
When?
Delivery Milestone
Each model tied to a programme milestone — a binding delivery schedule, not a wish list.

Common Errors
5 MPDT Mistakes to Avoid
1
LOI missing — only LOD defined
LOD defines geometry. LOI defines data. Without LOI, models arrive empty of parameters — useless for FM and asset management.
2
Created once, never updated
The MPDT is a living document. Every scope, team or programme change must trigger a new revision.
3
Unrealistic LOD targets per stage
LOD 400 at Stage 2 wastes resources. Calibrate LOD to decisions needed at each milestone, not the final product.
4
No milestones — just LOD targets
Without delivery dates tied to the programme, the MPDT is a list, not a contract. Every row needs a when.
5
Authored by BIM Manager alone
Each Appointed Party must validate their own entries. A solo-authored MPDT always has scope, software, or timeline errors.
