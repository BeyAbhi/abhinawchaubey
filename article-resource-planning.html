Resource Planning
in BIM

A practical guide to forecasting, managing, and monitoring BIM manpower across project stages — from mobilisation through to construction — ensuring that delivery commitments in the MIDP and MPDT are always backed by sufficient team capacity.

ISO 19650-2 Manpower Planning 2026 Edition
Definition
A BIM Resource Planning Register is a structured forecast of manpower demand across all project stages and disciplines — mapping the number of full-time equivalents (FTEs) required per role, per stage, against confirmed team availability. It is the tool that ensures MIDP and MPDT commitments are deliverable — not just planned.
Who?
Roles & Skills
Maps specific BIM roles — Information Manager, BIM Lead, Coordinator, Author — to the deliverables they own at each stage, matching skill to task.
How Much?
FTE Demand
Quantifies demand in full-time equivalents per role per stage — converting MIDP deliverable volume and deadlines into concrete headcount requirements.
When?
Stage Peaks
Identifies peak demand periods — typically at SD-to-DD and CD-to-IFC transitions — where resource gaps must be resolved through recruitment, subcontracting, or scope phasing.
Gap?
Variance Tracking
Tracks the gap between demand and confirmed availability — triggering early escalation before shortfalls become missed milestones and contractual risk.
Team Roles
6 Core BIM Roles in the Resource Plan
🏛️
Information Manager
Governs the overall information management framework — BEP, MIDP, MPDT, audit, CDE. Typically 0.5–1.0 FTE throughout the project lifecycle.
📐
BIM Lead / Coordinator
Leads day-to-day coordination, clash management, federated model governance, and team upskilling. Peaks at DD and IFC stages.
✏️
BIM Author
Produces model content — architectural, structural, MEP, civil — to agreed LOD. Demand is highest during DD and CD when most model content is authored.
⚙️
BIM Technician
Supports model production with family creation, template management, naming convention enforcement, and routine QA/QC tasks.
📊
Digital / Data Engineer
Manages automation, Dynamo scripting, Power BI dashboards, ACC configuration, and data extraction workflows across the project.
🔍
QA / Audit Lead
Conducts independent model audits at stage gates, manages comment resolution, and maintains the audit register. Typically part-time with audit peaks at each gate.

Common Errors
5 Resource Planning Mistakes
1
Plan built without MIDP as the demand basis
Resource plans built from estimates rather than MIDP deliverable counts are always wrong. FTE demand must flow directly from the number and complexity of deliverables committed to in the MIDP.
2
Peak stages not identified until it is too late
CD and IFC transitions are consistently underestimated. Resource peaks at these stages must be flagged at mobilisation — not discovered mid-stage when recruitment lead times make resolution impossible.
3
Subcontract onboarding time not factored in
A subcontractor needs 4–6 weeks to onboard — CDE access, BEP briefing, template setup, coordination process. Flagging a gap 2 weeks before a stage deadline is not resource planning.
4
Register treated as a one-time document
Resource requirements change as scope, programme, and team evolve. The register must be updated at every stage gate — at minimum monthly — and variance from plan tracked and explained.
5
All roles assumed to be full-time on the project
Information Managers, QA Leads, and Digital Engineers are rarely full-time on a single project. Actual availability — not contracted title — must be confirmed and reflected in the FTE figures.
