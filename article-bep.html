ISO 19650-2:2018 Pre & Post BEP 2026 Edition
Definition
The BIM Execution Plan (BEP) is the primary governance and delivery framework document for a project — defining BIM objectives, applicable standards, software platforms, CDE structure, delivery workflows, and the responsibilities of all Appointed Parties. It is the binding agreement between all parties on how BIM will be produced, managed, and delivered across the project lifecycle.
What?
BIM Objectives
Defines what the project aims to achieve through BIM — use cases, information requirements, asset data outcomes, and the client's specific digital delivery goals.
How?
Standards & Methods
Specifies the standards, software, file formats, naming conventions, and workflows that all Appointed Parties must follow — creating a consistent delivery environment.
Who?
Roles & Responsibilities
Defines the Lead Appointed Party, all Appointed Parties, and the Appointing Party — with clear responsibilities for every BIM function across the project lifecycle.
When?
Delivery Schedule
Links to the MIDP and MPDT — establishing the information delivery timeline, stage gate milestones, and exchange requirements from mobilisation to handover.
Two Versions
Pre-Appointment vs Post-Appointment BEP
Pre-Appointment BEP
The Tender Response
Issued by the bidding team in response to the EIR during the tender process. Demonstrates capability, proposed approach, and compliance with the client's information requirements. Used by the client to evaluate BIM competence before awarding the contract.
Proposed BIM uses and objectives
Outline team structure and competencies
Indicative software and CDE platform
High-level MIDP and delivery approach
Risk and opportunity register — initial
Post-Appointment BEP
The Binding Document
Developed collaboratively after contract award — confirming all delivery details, workflows, and commitments agreed between the Lead AP, Appointed Parties, and the client. This is the version that governs delivery. It must be finalised before any model authoring begins.
Confirmed team, roles, and responsibilities
Full CDE structure and access matrix
Complete MIDP and MPDT attached
LOD / LOI schedule per stage confirmed
QA/QC procedures and audit schedule

Common Errors
5 BEP Mistakes to Avoid
1
BEP issued after modelling has already started
The BEP must be agreed and issued before any model authoring begins. Starting without an approved BEP means every file produced lacks a governance basis — and retrofitting standards mid-project is costly and incomplete.
2
Pre-appointment BEP copied as the post-appointment BEP
The pre-appointment BEP is a proposal. The post-appointment BEP is a contract. Copying one as the other without developing the full delivery detail — CDE setup, MIDP, MPDT, team structure — results in a governance document that cannot actually govern anything.
3
BEP not updated at stage gates
A BEP that is issued at mobilisation and never updated is a snapshot, not a governance document. It must be reviewed and revised at every stage gate — reflecting scope changes, team changes, and programme revisions.
4
MIDP and MPDT not attached
A BEP without the MIDP and MPDT as appendices is incomplete. These documents are what make the BEP deliverable — without them, the governance framework has no delivery schedule to enforce.
5
Appointed Parties not involved in BEP development
A BEP authored by the Information Manager alone and imposed on sub-consultants will always contain errors on software, LOD, and delivery timelines. Every Appointed Party must contribute to and validate their section before the BEP is finalised.
